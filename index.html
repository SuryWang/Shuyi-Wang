About

My current research examines the English translations and international circulation of the Chinese sociological term Chaxugeju in academic discourse. I am particularly interested in how Chinese academic terminology is translated, circulated, and received in international scholarly communication.

My broader research interests lie at the intersection of translation studies, academic discourse analysis, and international communication.

    • A Research Report on the English Translation of Chinese Shadow Puppetry Terms

    Team Leader · Final Research Report for Terminology Translation Course · Sep 2024 – Mar 2025

    Led a team project on the English translation of Chinese shadow puppetry terms, focusing on terminology translation, intangible cultural heritage, and international cultural communication.

    • Built a knowledge framework of Chinese shadow puppetry and classified core terms across seven categories.
    • Evaluated existing English renderings based on accuracy, readability, systematicity, transparency, distinctiveness, conciseness, professionalism, and cultural transmission.
    • Summarized translation strategies including transliteration, free translation, contextualized translation, hybrid translation, and system-based compound translation.
